Our first time on the trail we asked where to stop and get food, the bike shop owner suggested Loacker. We assumed it was a cafe with really good “biscuits,” we’re so American. We were on the look-out going through every town the trail took us to. When we found it we were shocked that it was not a cafe. Loacker makes sweets, biscuits meaning, wafer. This was a brand name, it was their distribution/headquarters. Think on the size of Keebler elves, we had a good laugh because we had no idea. Going inside it is sweets galore. Michelle and I started filling up a basket with these “ware-house direct prices” sweets we didn’t even know existed 5 minutes ago. When the lady rang up €30 we had our “oh shit” moment. We should have had lunch in their café before buying the sweets.

Going back outside we fill up on gelato then fill up our water bottles. You’ll know you’re getting closer to Austria when there are more languages on the signs. The Italian influence on architecture becomes only the terra-cotta roofing. The mountains begin to dwindle, this part you're more or less in the woods rather than out in the open. Not to say it isn’t beautiful because it most certainly is. Do not fret because at some parts you’re right at the foot of more mountains.

When you cross the border and make it into Lienz you feel a great deal of accomplishment. Riding 25 miles from one country to another, riding through the mountains, and the woods. Welcoming you is the “Benvenuti A Lienz” sign. Ask to take the picture for a group, and they will return the favor. After you get the perfect angle hop back on your bike and follow the signs to the city center.

Lienz is a beautiful city. So many people from all over come to visit. You can have lunch at any number of restaurants or cafe’s. There are some really talented street performers, some of which have very “unique” acts. There is great shopping too, this is where I bought my beloved Bugatti Italian Leather boots. I wear those suckers all over Europe. Once you're done head back to the train station.

This is one of our favorite trips it requires all the senses, pumps the blood and makes you stay mindful. We will without a doubt take every visitor on this trip. Michelle’s mom had a great time and said it was an easy ride. Also if you are a dependent and waiting for your Soggiorno and can’t leave Italy at the time there are no guards on the trail doing passport checks so go have some fun and explore.
