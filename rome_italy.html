Holy Rolling in Rome

Rome, Italy | July 24, 2018

Rome, the "eternal city" the one city that would always be standing even as the others fall. Rome is huge, there is so much to do and see which in my mind makes it the best city in Italy to start with. You can spend hours roaming the streets, walking 9 miles is the norm. After a full day of exploring, food, wine, you won’t have any problem getting over the jet-lag.

Our first trip began to Rome was during our honey moon. For whatever reason that I cannot remember now we were going to fly into Naples, go to Rome then go South to Amalfi. Once we arrived to Montgomery airport we realized that our flight was being delayed by 45 minutes. For some reason this is the norm for Montgomery airport each trip we are delayed at least once.

Boarding the airplane we were told we would be arriving an hour later to Atlanta Airport. When we land we have 15 minutes to get to our plane, 15 minutes to find our flight, to run to the terminal. So Atlanta airport is huge, so huge it has a subway to get you to the different terminals. We get off the plane and run, we’re booking it, dodging people, sweating, breathing heavily. We get to the terminal and we’re late. Only nine minutes late, you can still see the plane with the bridge connected but the door is closed. Now the Hartings have to do the walk of shame to the international concierge and find a new flight.

What a way to kick off our honey moon. Standing in a long line, hoping there would be a flight available that could still get us to Italy in the morning like our first flight. The lady working the counter is nice, and tells us there is a flight to Rome that would land an hour later than our original flight. “There’s only one problem” she said “Your final destination is Naples, and not Rome.” They couldn’t change our final destination, but the next flight to Naples would be way out of the way. While the lady was trying to make it work Michelle laid it on thick. Michelle said “this is for our honeymoon,” “oh your son is active duty? So am I.” This is a gift Michelle has, before you know it they’re talking like friends and we have tickets to Rome.

Nine hours later we land in Rome, the time is 10:45. This airport is huge, and once again we run into some bad luck. Even though we missed our flight to Naples some of our luggage didn’t. We’re missing two bags, so here is Harting’s tip numero uno pack a change of clothes in your carry on. You never know when the airlines may lose your luggage. The lady working informed us our luggage would be shipped to the hotel next day. Now it came time to get to the Hotel. It’s now passed 12 and the hotel shuttle would not come to pick us up the man said “sorry sir that is impossible.”

I’ll be honest I can’t remember how we got to the hotel. Now living here I know how easy it is to take the train. You take the train in the airport to Roma Termini then find the station closest to your destination and hop on that one. Some how we arrived to the hotel using Google Maps without roaming. When we get to the room there was a knock at our door and they gave us complimentary champagne for our honeymoon. We pop the cork and despite the lack of luck getting here, we cheers to the beginning of a great trip.

Talking to concierge they informed us that the coliseum, being it was the first Sunday of the month, was free to enter. They handed us a map then activated the free gps phone for us and we were on our way. It was June, the sun was shining, car horns were chirping like birds, and we were walking. This is what you do in Rome, you walk, make sure to bring a really good pair of shoes. As you walk closer and closer to the Coliseum you have more and more people coming up to you. “The coliseum is this way, do you have your tickets?” “skip the line come this way my friend.” At one point they will have badges saying they are official tour ministers of Italy, that’s when I can’t tell if they’re bullshitting me still or not. Either way we planned ahead and didn’t need the assistance.

Piazza Del Colosseo is huge and you gotta take a minute to stop and appreciate all that is around you. The trees the architecture, the people, everyone is bebopping around. Tours are happening all around you, guys are selling water, selfie sticks, guys are trying to put bracelets on your wrist and then demand money. I have to laugh because they tricked me last time we went. A man was trying to talk/sell me something, as I am ignoring him he says “what you don’t like black people.” It caught me so off guard that I turned around, only to be intercepted by his accomplice “sir, sir, he didn’t mean it, please forgive him, here take this.” He begins to put a wrist band on me and asks “can you spare some money.” Michelle is frustrated with me that I fell for it, it happened so fast, she told me to”give it back.” Just stay alert, and ignore everyone.

We wait a short amount of time to enter, and it’s shorter than you would expect with it being free. Walking In you’re filled with excitement. My brain was just going, going, going, you begin remembering what this place was used for. All the people who died fighting for their freedom, and others entertainment. It’s a really weird feeling being here, taking selfies, and enjoying the day. But now it was time for a selfie. Something we always do is offer to take the picture for a couple. It’s a nice gesture that leads to people returning the favor. We find a good spot with a couple taking a picture, we offer our services, “I count down 3, 2, 1 snap. I take a few picture, a few, like any human would do. Now it’s time for our picture as, we’re getting ready she snaps our picture. We look at the photo as they walk away and I’m not smiling. This is now one of my favorite photos.

One night we did a food tour, and the Hartings will always recommend you do a food tour, and make it one of the first things you do. Chances are you’ll go in the non-touristy areas, with the best food. So as we’re walking to the meeting point I decide I need an espresso. I ask for it to be “porta via,” “take away” the barista looks at me like I just committed treason. She puts it in a small plastic cup and it’s nearly melting by the time we walk out the door. We meet at train station and we’re one of the first to arrive.

Once we all get there we do a meet and greet. Our tour guide said she was originally from another European country, she studied at Rome and has stayed ever since. Then the oldest couple of the group introduce themselves, they’re from Australia. The older man does most of talking for both of them, he spoke like Steve Irwin, and dressed like Crocodile Dundee on vacation. Then another couple from Australia speaks, they’re around our age and have no accents I didn’t catch at first either. We start hanging out with each other during the tour and the old man called the young one mate. Under his breath he said “i’m not your mate, mate” I’m floored my whole world is rocked. I was under the assumption all Australians talked like Steve Irwin. He explained how that accent is the equivalent of a southern accent in America, so is that kind of living. Now thinking about it if Steve Irwin were alive and American he’d would be Florida man.

Every stop we make has the best food ever. We tried prosciutto with cheese, paired with wine. We had pizza made from dough that rose for 72 hours. The dinner was amazing and we were stuffed by then. The restaurant brought out a bottle of Prosecco for our honey moon and the whole table had some. What an experience. After dinner of course it was time for gelato. We had a crash course in gelato 101. We were informed on what are the signs of fake gelato… Bananas are not yellow, pistachios are not green, neither should be your gelato. Gelato is thick, and shouldn’t be piled above the container. Cookies and cream, or bright blue flavors are not gelato norms. Remember these facts and you will find the good stuff. Though in Rome the food quality is always poor in the tourist areas.

The vatican, its huge, full of art, history, texts, and there is gold thread in the carpets that hang on the wall. There is so much you can’t see it in one day. We have been twice now, what I can tell you is the tour guide will make or brake the experience. Our first trip had the best tour guide, she was born to do be a guide. She was American and studied art history in Rome and decided to stay. Our second tour was hosted by a Frenchman who was trying to speak English. He talked so softly you thought he was afraid to wake the excitement within. Two different experiences, two different companies. I will focus on the first tour.

We went with CitySightseeing Roma, you can find it by looking for the big red double decker busses. Our tour came with a guide and “skip the line,” it is absolutely needed in peek tourist season, not so much during off tourist season. Upon entering you are given a receiver and headphones to wear allowing you to hear your tour guide amongst all the noise, it’s noisy, and there are a lot of people 5 million a year, up to 20,000 a day just in the sistine chapel. It’s so many that guards will rope off rooms, and reopen others all day just to keep the flow going.

Our tour guide did not focus on the aspect of religion as much as the history, what the art meant, and how it affected Italy. Her catchphrase was “everything came together, Hunky-Dory Kumbaya.” She talked in emphasis on the art, I mean that was her degree, it’s fascinating to hear the details. She spoke with emphasis and passion, you could tell she loved doing this and she was in her flow. At the end we were so happy with her we wanted to give her a tip, she out-right refused saying “go buy a beer and cheers in my name.” I was so disappointed with the second tour, I really couldn’t tell you any facts from him.

That was our honeymoon trip to Rome, and a little bit of the second. What an experience to be in such an ancient city. To walk where some of the most famous people have. To touch the foot of a statue as millions have before you. It really puts your life in perspective that we’re all just a really small part of something larger, whether it’s the Earth, Universe, or Religion, you will feel something visiting Rome, and the Vatican.
