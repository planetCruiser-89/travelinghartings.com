Rest & Relaxation Along Lake Bled

Lake Bled, Slovenia | July 24, 2018

This trip the Hartings planned a weekend retreat to Slovenia, a smaller country that you can drive to from Italy. Slovenia feels like a mix of Italy and Austria. Tis the Carnival season and Slovenia has their own festivities in full swing. Mainly happening in the major cities of Slovenia we chose not to go. After some research Michelle found Lake Bled, only a two and a half mile drive from home. On Airbnb we found a cozy little house, it had a fire place, was dog friendly, and walking distance from the lake. The house was nested along the forest edge. Talking to some friends over a couple of Aperol Spritz’s they told us where to go and what to do.

There was a commanders call Friday, and Michelle was being awarded for the hard work she did back at Maxwell. Though we had done the ceremony once she was actually being awarded with the real decoration this time. Our new friends, who are in a similar situation, male spouse, and a female active duty who was also being awarded. It is really cool seeing my wife be awarded for all the handwork she does. It is also cool I get to witness this part of the Air Force as well. The last time I went to a commander’s call Intel gave a briefing. I was barred from entering and those who could had to leave their phones and smart watches at the door. Later that night we went to I-sushi to celebrate. I-sushi is one of our favorite restaurants in Pordenone, and you rarely need a reservation for two.

After sleeping in a bit we loaded the mini with clothes, wine and puppies. In Italy the highway is called the Autostrada the speed limit is 130 km/h or 80 mp/h which to be honest is a suggestion. It is two lanes wide and the left is strictly for passing if you need to pass you need to do it fast, unless someone will be right on your ass.

One of the tips we got from our friends for this trip was to avoid the highways to avoid the fees. The autostrada has tolls, Austrian highways require a vignette as well as Slovenian highways. That’s quite a bit of cash. For this trip -or any trip- make certain you switch on “avoid highways” in Google Maps (instructions below). Besides it is an amazing drive with so me spectacular sights. We did not find this feature until on our way back, we relied on friends directions and ended up on a Slovenian highway for 6 minutes.

traveling Northwards we marveled at the enormous snow-capped Alps. We felt the cold crisp air seeping through the cracked-open sunroof. The warm sun laying on our skin the mild scent of fresh mountain air. Our conversation gravitated to the fact how fortunate we are to live here. Some of the streets were freshly plowed and demanded a little more of my attention. Further North we crossed through skiing towns, the cars lined the street. skiers looked like ants as they cruised down the mountain.

The Hartings arrived at Lake Bled and decided to do a drive around the lake to get a feel for the area. Some of you may remember this being a mistake in Amsterdam due to all the bikes and small roads, but not for Lake Bled this was a pleasant drive. Lake Bled demands full attention measuring a whopping 6,960 ft by 4,530 ft with a max depth of 97 ft. A small island named Blejski otok or Bled Island on it a church erected in honor of the Assumption of Mary stands tall. Looking towards the distant mountains you see Bled Castle towering above on a rock face. What a sight to see these elements interacting wit each other.

The Hartings were in awe, the puppies were restless all of us were ready to get out and stretch. We punched in the address to our airbnb titled “A lovely house with a garden at the edge of the forest (LINK).” Driving up the gravel road the Hartings knew they made the right choice. The house felt like a wooded retreat, and required only a short walk to Lake Bled. We pull up to the house, open the car, and the puppies scramble to sniff the area. It’s nice to let them run of the leash because they are always on it in Pordenone.

Our hosts Johnny & Rachel are a younger couple who definitely watched that Netflix series about how to run a successful airbnb. Entering the house we notice two croissants, and 100% organic fruit and vegetable juice on the table. The croissants are filled with apricot jam, and the juice was amazing. This is a cute and cozy house. A fire place had logs ready to burn, the heater was already on, it was perfect. Johnny & Rachel supplied many books, and board games on a shelf next to the fire place. They hung a projector from the ceiling with a screen by the dining table. Next to the couch were Johnny & Rachel’s local recommendations complete with descriptions, numbers, and addresses. A great touch especially to aid tourists to steer clear of the major traps. Looking around we were satisfied with our decision and poured a few glasses of wine.

The walk to Lake Bled gave us such an appreciation for the area, there were mountains in the distance. The houses and architecture were beautiful. The dogs were happy to be out and about. We come to the main Lake Bled entryway there are souvenir shops, clothing stores, antiques, and cafes. Feeling hungry we went to a friend recommended restaurant, not by our hosts. Walking up to Oštarija Peglez’n you first notice across the street on the lake side, a restaurant adorned with a beautiful outdoor seating area overlooking Lake Bled. We were told by the same friends it was not as good. Which usually you pay for the view with lesser quality food. However we should get our cakes there.

We inside Oštarija Peglez’n with the dogs and right away they bring a water bowl for them. We order some draft beers, they tasted like cheap American light beers. We start with some soups I order the fish soup, Michelle ordered soup of the day, a delicious broccoli soup. We order the fish platter the description reads “for 2” and it only costs €38. They place it on a hot plate warmed by tea-cup candles. It’s not a platter it’s a feast. Complete with fried calamari, fried octopus, 2 grilled fillets, grilled squid, 2 whole prawn, vegetables and potatoes. The Hartings were delighted, and had to take most to go.

Full bellies and a new hunger to explore the Hartings hit the trail. Lake bled boasts a 6km or 3.728 mile path around the lake. It is paved but could benefit from being just a little wider. Especially because today there was a marathon with plenty of people jogging the lake, multiple times, in addition to all the other active tourists. Quick note don’t rent a bike it looked miserable. While circling the lake take note how the government has responsibly controlled the land use. The forest still creeps up to the trail, and hotels are built on the other side of the street. The land is still mostly unboned and kept natural. Lake Bled is a photographers dream there are so many elements, and you have the entire path to angle your shot. Every 10 steps I would say to Michelle “this is the last time.” I’m grateful she would stop each time. What I really loved taking pictures of was the church, what a subject. A tiny island with a rising steeple.

We decided to wait until the next day to visit the island. There are three ways of getting to the island. Option one you can pay €12 to ride on the Pletna boat -think of a bigger Gondola- and then you can explore the island for about 30 minutes. Option two rent your own three-person row boat for €15/hour or five-person for €20/hour. Option three you can swim to the island. We didn’t see anyone swimming but it was still cold. Note: if you swim you are not permitted to enter the church unless you bring dry clothes.

The church is known for hosting weddings and the tradition goes you carry your significant other up the 99 steps for good luck. So have your wheaties for breakfast. Once you enter the church you may wonder around. But you have to walk up the bell tower. Newlyweds would make a wish and ring the bell 3 times. Don’t worry you can still do this anytime. This costs €6 an adult or €1 a kid.

The Hartings finally come to the end of the walk, the pups are tired and happy. Looking at my watch it reads cake o’ clock. That’s right Lake Bled is home to the famous Kremna Rezina or the Kremšnita. This cake is legendary. The lady that owns the cafe next to us in Pordenone told me about the cake, saying it’s “as big as an American cake.”

The Kremšnita’s creator Ištvan Lukačević first started preparing it in 1953 at the Hotel Park's patisserie. This recipe was declared a protected dish of designated origin in 2016 by the Slovene government. It’s a big deal. No matter where you went it was the same cake made by the same bakery assuring you were getting the original Kremšnita. Hotel Park’s patisserie has prepared 12 million cakes during the past 60 years, that’s a lot of cake. We go to the restaurant from earlier to buy two cakes and take them to go.

This cake is big, I mean really big. You need a fork for sure. The cake consists of a thick vanilla custard base, with whipped cream on top, sandwich between two thin puff pastry crackers, and topped with powdered sugar. It has a very light taste, so light you don’t regret having it. Like most European sweets, it’s not overly sweet.

The Hartings get cleaned up start the fire and open another bottle of wine. We try to use the projector but without USB to HDMI we couldn’t get the projector to work. No worries we just watched a movie from the phone. The dogs, exhausted from the walk had melted into lumps. The Hartings, cuddled on the couch. The fire, warm and cozy. Life, amazing and as it should be.

Our original plans were to see about getting a massage at hotel Rikli, recommended by a friend who was a massage therapist. Then depending on the time of the massage paddle out to Bled Island before or after. I would rather have the massage after rowing the boat and carrying wifey up 99 stairs. But while looking at their offers we made a big decision. We would skip the island and be pampered all day. For only €180 The Hartings would experience the Alpine program for two. We began disrobing and donning whit cotton robes, we were lead into a dark room with a huge jacuzzi. For 40 minutes we bathed in essential oils from mountain herbs in a dark room. It was so relaxing

Foregoing the swimming pool we headed for the Sauna, we were told you would be naked. But they gave you a sheet to wear if you wanted. I have never been in a sauna but it was amazing. There were about 5 different sauna rooms, there were two steam rooms, a tiny swimming area, outdoor jacuzzi that bordered the edge, and one of those pine saunas with rocks you could pour water onto to increase the humidity. What an experience, I normally hate sweating but I loved this. After an hour it was time to head back up for our 50 minute massage.

This being my second massage ever I can safely say if you have never had one you are missing out. Do yourself a favor go out and get one, you will not be sorry. We were lead down a hallway by a young man and a tall older woman. Michelle and the woman were in front and when the woman said you may enter Michelle was next to her and I was next to the guy. The man asks me “you’re coming with me?” That’s when Michelle turned around and saw the look of horror on his and I’s faces. We had a laugh and I went with the woman. She was tall and had big hands and she knew what she was doing. Michelle’s masseuse was respectful of her body and always checked to make sure she was comfortable. Within minutes I was transported to another dimension.

50 minutes went by fast we were done feeling centered and rejuvenated. All we had to do was walk back 12 minutes and we could fully relax. Walking out of the Hotel Rikli we felt balanced like one with the Univers. Time slowed down during our ascent to the house, all felt right and whole. The dogs were excited to see us and once again we opened a bottle of wine and just existed next to one another in our cozy little airbnb.

Michelle found the restaurant Gostilna Pri Planincu or Inn at the Mountain. The restaurant was also suggested by Johnny and Rachel. Gostilna Pri Planincu is located in what is described as the old part of Lake Bled which is always a good sign when searching for authentic local foods. It was only a twenty minute walk and we brought the pups. We didn’t know if they would be welcomed but they haven’t been barred from any store yet.

When entering you get that old pub vibe, not to say it looked old but you know it was a staple of the area. One room was a pub the other side was the dining area. The back was a tiny kitchen with one older lady working. The upstairs was it’s own pizza place and I assume at one time was the inn. Right away the waitress welcomed us in along with our dogs. She even said one of her dogs looked like Michonne, meaning she got some extra loving from the waitress. We sit down and the waitress brings out a water bowl for the dogs. Never in America am I right? Looking at the menu I see Budweiser beer, but from the Czech Republic. Thinking of Grandpa who has been a bud man all his life I had to order one. It comes in a big green bottle and it tasted like a full body Budweiser. We order a plate of local cheeses, and some Goulash soup to start.

It’s all amazing I even stated how the soup tastes home-made something the waitress says about the food later on. For dinner we split a beefsteak with mushroom sauce and potatoes. I order medium rare not knowing that is basically what America calls rare. It’s delicious but I we ask to cook it just a little longer. Delicious. Now it’s time for dessert, Michelle can gauge people pretty well and asks if the dessert is worth it or if the shop we read about was better. The waitress explained how everything is home made and she highly recommends it. We ordered the apple strudel with cinnamon ice cream, and whipped cream. It was better than the apple pie in Amsterdam. The Hartings highly recommend Gostilna Pri Planincu it really blew the first restaurant, and cream cake out of the water. Full and happy the Hartings decide to walk back to our cozy little house, sit by the fire and finish Saturday night’s movie.

We started the day by packing the car and cleaning up the house a bit. We leave a bottle of wine for Johnny & Rachel, something we have never done but it felt right. They put in the extra work to make sure our stay was as good as possible. On the way back I changed the google map’s settings to correctly take us on a route that would avoid all highways. After a quick espresso we’re on the road. Puppies sleeping, wifey sleeping, and Matt driving, yep it’s a Harting road trip.
