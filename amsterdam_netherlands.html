Slinking Around in the Red Lights

Amsterdam, Netherlands | July 24, 2018

Reunited once again, this would be our last trip together while Michelle completes her 3 month long manning assist in Spanghdalem, Germany. This is a trip I was waiting for quite some time now. This would be my first trip to Amsterdam, and Michelle’s second trip. From food tours, to a live sex show, here is what we did in Amsterdam.

My flight started in Treviso airport at 14:55 and I would land in Frankfurt Main at 16:20. This was the cheapest flight I have ever taken costing a whopping €23 (about $26) to get there. Now Ryanair is the no frills airline. Ryanair will try to nickel and dime you any way that they can. But they really are your best option, especially if you want to use all them euros on your experiences.

I don’t do well with sitting at home waiting for a flight so I left early for the airport. Each time I have went to Treviso the security line was very long. Just remember even on “30 Rock” Jack (Alec Baldwin) says “there are no rules, it’s like check-in at an Italian airport.” It’s true, I have seen people cut lines. I have seen people crowd the boarding gate like it’s a cafe counter. I even saw someone smoking on a plane on the way to Dublin (you can read about that here(Link Dublin page).

ON THE WAY BACK WE HAD TO WAIT 10 minutes to board because we beat the crew to the plane. So here I am packed in a shuttle bus like a barrel of herrings. Minimal wind standing at the door staring at the plane.

So I land in Frankfurt, and like last time I have to board a shuttle to get to the terminal. There is snow on the ground and I am getting nervous. I have a two-and-a-half hour drive to Spanghdalem and the temperature is near freezing. The next step was to get my rental car. This is when I realized I made a mistake. When I talked to the gentleman working he informed me the car was rented out. My pick-up time was for 10:00, which on their website is the default time. I didn’t switch it because you would think if you reserve the car that day they would wait. So I had to get the next model size up and pay €100 more. Oh well, learn from my mistakes. Learn from my mistakes. Next I grabbed a bite to eat I sat there watching some dude sitting in front of KFC wearing headphones, smoking a cigarette, and acting like he was in a rap video. The older lady next to him looked in amazement. I just couldn’t believe he was smoking in the middle of the food court. It was entertaining enough.

Fast forward to Spanghdalem there is snow on the ground and it’s dark. This base is big compared to Aviano, and more spread out. I get to Michelle’s and everything is good again. We had not seen each other since January second. We don’t stay up too late and we get to bed to start an early day. After a quick workout we get some road trip snacks (because road trip calories don’t count). Amsterdam is only a 4 hour drive from base and Michelle has a diesel rental car, and one tank lasted there and back.

We arrive in Amsterdam and go for a drive through the city. The first thing you notice about Amsterdam are the bikes. Bikes are everywhere, regular bikes, bikes with seats for kids, bikes with front-end buggies for multiple kids, electric bikes, bikes, bikes, and more bikes. The Dutch love bikes, in-fact I would highly suggest not driving into the city. When you go off a main road you wonder if you’re in a bike lane or not. The Dutch love their bikes, and it shows. Just to give you a reference there are 881,000 bikes in Amsterdam, that’s 4 times as many cars. So the Harting’s suggest you do the park and go.

Amsterdam is well known for their public transportation system. While you are walking you will see the street trolleys, busses, metro, and train system. The vast network makes you wonder why anyone would actually own a car. From the airport to the city center is about an 18-30 minute ride on the metro system. It is very cheap and we highly suggest it rather than taking a taxi, which will run you quite a bit more money. So what if you drive into Amsterdam like the Hartings? Well Amsterdam has you covered.

This is where the Hartings messed up, but you will learn, and save from our mistakes. Amsterdam offers the Park and Ride discount which I will link to here (https://www.iamsterdam.com/en/plan-your-trip/getting-around/parking/park-and-ride). There are a few stipulations, which we did not follow and ended up paying for it in the end. If you are able to complete the requirements then you parking will cost the change you have left over. If you fail to fulfill them it will cost €60 like it did for the Hartings, and yes it still hurts.

We also had trouble with the public transportation because we purchased a ticket for the train rather than the metro, both were in the same station. We were looking at the train times confused on why we could not find our destination. After a few attempts we got it right, and after a transfer we were at our airbnb.

The Hartings do not look for luxury airbnbs when we go on city center trips. We look for location, bed size and price, everything else is fluff and extra price. That is why we chose the Rembrandt Studio 1 it was perfect the bed was comfy, the space was clean, it was our pit stop for our weekend trip. That’s all you need when staying in a city.

When veering off the main road into the alley we were greeted by the owner’s friend. As he opens the building’s door he leads us up the stairs Let me just point out Amsterdam builds up and has minimal space to work with. So when looking at the stairs it’s like looking up Everest. The stairs are so narrow you’ll notice hooks at the tops of buildings that are used for hoisting furniture in through the windows at the top. With the Dutch being so tall you have to wonder how many times they have hit their heads on the stairway arches.

The room is beautiful, it’s cozy and well lit, the radiator was more than enough for the place and we only kept it on when awake. Our main window gave us a pleasant view of the brickwork of the building across the alley. Looking down you could watch the people mosey on by and at night you could hear the debauchery taking place in the alley. But like I stated we came to Amsterdam for the streets not the room, which was still great. All in all I would stay here again, and if not for Ruben we would have gotten off at a station further down than what we needed.

Ever since I could drink I enjoyed Heineken, the green bottle, the distinct taste at least from what other twenty-somethings were drinking at the time. And of course Hunter S. Thompson, the Gonzo journalist drank Heineken. Michelle missed this part of the trip her first time to Amsterdam allowing us to “experience” it together. The Hartings have toured two other breweries the Anheuser-Busch brewery in St. Louis, and the Guinness brewery in Dublin (you can read about that here).
